From the IB Coordinator

May 2009


Attention Team Hillsborough IB 2008-2009:

I want to stress to all IB stakeholders the importance of being at school every day for the remainder of the year. We have many important academic activities on schedule for the rest of the year. Whether it is CAS, junior oral exams, semester projects or exams, everyday is critical for the remainder of the semester. All absences must still be reported to student affairs. Any unexcused absence still carries a penalty of two points off a student’s final average.

Let’s finish strong. Summer is almost here . . . but not here yet!!

Go Big Red!!

Mr. Jones

From the IB Counselor

SPRING UPDATE !

Advanced Placement (AP) Testing update - Students will receive information about testing times and locations between May 16 and 21. Freshmen, sophomores and juniors will also receive a permission slip that must be signed and returned by Thursday, April 30, 2009, if your child will use school business on exam day(s) for the non-testing time.

C/O 2009
Congratulations to all of our seniors who have been admitted to colleges. While most students are choosing a Florida school this year we have had a good acceptance percentage across the board. I will provide an update after I have received information from all students. Congratulations to all! There is a mandatory meeting for all seniors and their parent(s) to discuss testing procedures on Tuesday, April 21, at 6:30 pm in the cafeteria. Please make plans to attend.

C/O 2010
Juniors, I have met with many of you concerning your college plans. Thanks, parents, for taking the time to meet with me. Remember that I work during the summer so that you can email, call or stop by with college application questions. I can also read your essays to talk to you about the topics so that you can get started. The college essay is your one chance to become a person instead of a number (GPA and test score). Be creative yet not off the wall. Use this summer to visit colleges and start your applications.

C/O 2011
Sophomores, if you are looking for something to do this summer, why not start on your IB CAS hours. Summer is a great chance to get involved in a project that meets a community need. You should choose something that holds an interest to you AND serves the community.

C/O 2012
Freshmen, it is hard to believe that you have already almost completed your first year at HHS. Many of you have learned new skills such as studying and balancing your time. Congratulations on your accomplishments.
